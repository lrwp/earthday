Earth Day Fort Wayne to expand in 2013

In just its second year, Earth Day Fort Wayne (a Little River Wetlands Project event) has become the preeminent Earth Day celebration in Northeast Indiana. Donâ€™t forget to mark your calendar to attend this popular festival on Sunday, April 21 from 1:00 to 5:00 p.m. at Eagle Marsh. The first annual event was a success, drawing more than 500 people, and this year is shaping up to be another fabulous event.

"We were thrilled with the turnout last year," says Sean Nolan, Executive Director of Little River Wetlands Project (LRWP). "I think it demonstrates that people in northeast Indiana are genuinely enthused about conservation and taking care of the planet."

The expanded offerings of the 2013 event will include fun and educational events for the entire family and is expected to draw nearly 1,500 people. We'll have public participation with native plantings at Eagle Marsh, nature education stations with area naturalists, activity tent with more than 20 area environmental organizations, earth-friendly marketplace, food vendors, 5K Walk fundraiser (registration required), children's activities including face painting and coloring contest, bike corral for bicyclists riding in on the Towpath Trail, and more.

For up to date information on all the different activities and participants, be sure to "like" our Earth Day Fort Wayne Facebook page at  Facebook.com/EarthDayFortWayne. Those interested in signing up for the 5K walk can do so by either registering online at  firstgiving.com/lrwp/EarthDayWalk  or downloading a registration form and mailing it to the LRWP office at 7209 Engle Road, Suite 200, Fort Wayne, IN 46804.

Special thanks to our sponsors: Aqua, General Motors, Indiana Michigan Power, NIPSCO, OmniSource, PHP, 3Rivers Federal Credit Union, Earth First, Fort Wayne Metals, and The Plex South. This incredible event wouldn't be possible without their support!
