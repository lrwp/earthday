Earth Day Fort Wayne to expand in 2013

In just its second year, Earth Day Fort Wayne (a Little River Wetlands Project event) has become the preeminent Earth Day celebration in Northeast Indiana. Don’t forget to mark your calendar to attend this popular festival on  Sunday, April 21st, from 1:00 p.m.   to  5:00 p.m.   at  Eagle Marsh. The first annual event was a success, drawing more than 500 people, and this year is shaping up to be another fabulous event.

"We were thrilled with the turnout last year," says Sean Nolan, Executive Director of Little River Wetlands Project (LRWP). "I think it demonstrates that people in northeast Indiana are genuinely enthused about conservation and taking care of the planet."

The expanded offerings of the 2013 event will include fun and educational events for the entire family and is expected to draw nearly 1,500 people. We'll have public participation with native plantings at Eagle Marsh, nature education stations with area naturalists, activity tent with more than 20 area environmental organizations, earth-friendly marketplace, food vendors, 5K Walk fundraiser (registration required), children's activities including face painting and coloring contest, bike corral for bicyclists riding in on the Towpath Trail, and more.

For up to date information on all the different activities and participants, be sure to "like" our Earth Day Fort Wayne Facebook page at  Facebook.com/EarthDayFortWayne . Those interested in signing up for the 5K walk can do so by either registering online at  firstgiving.com/lrwp/EarthDayWalk  or downloading a registration form and mailing it to the LRWP office at 7209 Engle Road, Suite 200, Fort Wayne, IN 46804.

Special thanks to our sponsors: Aqua, General Motors, Indiana Michigan Power, NIPSCO, OmniSource, PHP, 3Rivers Federal Credit Union, Earth First, Fort Wayne Metals, and The Plex South. This incredible event wouldn't be possible without their support!

Earth Day 5K Walk presented by OmniSource

The Earth Day 5K Walk is a unique fundraising walk that will take registered participants through the trails of Eagle Marsh while helping to raise much-needed funds to help pay for ongoing stewardship of Eagle Marsh and the free nature programming provided throughout the year. The walk (approximately 3.1 miles in length) will take about 50 minutes to complete. Take your time, enjoy the hike and know you are helping to make a difference with local conservation! Those interested in registering for this event can visit  firstgiving.com/lrwp/EarthDayWalk  to register. Registration is $20 for adults, $10 for youth ages 7-17, and free for children 6 and under. Adult and youth participants will receive a free commemorative Earth Day Fort Wayne T-shirt and goodie bag (no shirt or bags available for children 6 and under).

Native planting effort presented by NIPSCO

Join us as we plant more than 800 native plant varieties at Eagle Marsh. Volunteers will be on hand to help you make a difference for Eagle Marsh and the wildlife that rely on these important sources of food. Stop by the native planting area at Eagle Marsh from 1-5pm or until supplies last. Special thanks to NIPSCO for their help in making this happen.

GM Marketplace

For the first time, Earth Day Fort Wayne will feature an earth-friendly marketplace with vendors providing you the opportunity to purchase plant material for your home garden or landscape. Professionals will also be on hand to help answer those nagging questions you might have about what to plant and how to care for your garden. Participants will also have the opportunity to purchase many of the same native plants that are being installed as part of the native planting event during the festival.

Aqua Activity Tent

Be sure to stop by the Aqua Activity Tent to learn more about the impressive conservation work being done by more than 20 area nonprofits. Organizations will be on hand to answer questions you might have about water quality, land preservation, energy conservation, wildlife rehabilitation, importance of wetlands and more. Informative presentations on a number of earth-friendly topics will also take place throughout the day. Don’t forget to bring your children by the face-painting table so they can have their favorite animal displayed in style!

Nature Education Trail provided by Indiana Michigan Power

Ever wonder what life is like for the wildlife of Eagle Marsh? Be sure to bring your kids on this interesting and educational hike along one of the Eagle Marsh trails. Volunteer naturalists will have stations set up along the way providing fun and interactive ways to learn more about a variety of topics including frogs and toads, make a better bug, bee a pollinator, see a Salamander, and more! Kids will also have the opportunity to participate in a special coloring contest provided by Indiana Michigan Power.
